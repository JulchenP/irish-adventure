Feeling hungry?

Here's a collection of the best food-hotspots in Dublin!

↓

How about a bite to eat?

Before you head out for a pint (or a few) you need to fill your stomach with something delicious. To make the choice less complicated and to give you an idea of the different foods available to you, here's a list of my favorite restaurants all around the city.

Early Bird Menu

Something to consider when your appetite is large but your purse says broke – Early Bird Menus. Most restaurants offer them and I know you're familiar with the concept as we do occasionally have them in Germany also (e.g. at Bun Bites Beef). Dinner time starts around 6 pm in Ireland, so EBM are usally between 4 pm to 6 pm.

Whether it's a tradtional irish potato dish or a yummy burrito, you'll never go unsatisfied at these restaurants.

The Bernhard Shaw

There’s literally a big blue bus in the backyard of this pub. They serve pizza in there! Need I say more?

Cross Guns Bridge, Drumcondra, Dublin 9, D09 XW44, Irland

photo © https://thebigbluebusireland.wordpress.com/about/

Cornucopia

Ever feel like you need healthy, vegetarian food for a change? Me neither, but if you do, Cornucopia is the restaurant for you. It has a great selection of meals and the quality is outstanding.

19-20 Wicklow St, Dublin, D02 FK27, Irland

Brother Hubbard

I’ll let the brothers talk for this one:

„Our food is designed to offer nourishment and pleasure, and above all, you should feel better for having had it. That is not an experience that happens as often as any of us would like. We try to make pure food that is healthy and full of freshness, providing a feel-good quality without compromising on enjoyment. We don’t sermonise and ask you to change your lifestyle. We genuinely feel that this is food you might enjoy, and you might just happen to feel better after having eaten it.“

Delicious, healthy, Middle Eastern style. Perfect for breakfast, brunch, lunch and dinner.

LOCATION NORTH 153 Capel St, North City, Dublin 1, Irland

LOCATION SOUTH 46 Harrington St, Saint Kevin’s, Dublin 8, Irland

Mongolian Barbeque

Who cares about Irish Gastro Pubs. Sometimes you need something more exotic. At the Mongolian BBQ you can stuff your plate and hand it over to a chef, who will fire It up in front of you. Delicious!

7 Anglesea St, Temple Bar, Dublin 2, D02 K376, Irland

photo © https://thebigbluebusireland.wordpress.com/about/

Han Sung Asian Market

I know it says Asian Market in the title – believe me – this is the best and most authentic asian food you'll ever taste. It's far in the back of the market, just go through.

22 Strand Street Great, North City, Dublin, D01 K8E8, Ireland

Gallagher’s Boxty House

I’ve personally never been, but if you want to try out the best potato dishes in town you have to stop by the Boxty House. It’s the restaurant Phil Rosenthal went to in season 2 on „Somebody feed Phil“.

20-21 Temple Bar, Dublin 2, D02 ET66, Irland
Website

SoMa Restaurant

Voted No. 7 in Dublin’s best burger restaurants (Irish Independent).

Freshly made, locally sourced burgers and other delicacies (I heard the Mexican fish tacos are nice!) up for grabs! Get in there.

1 O'Connell Street Upper, North City, Dublin 1, D01 HR04, Ireland

White Moose Café

I’ve personally never been, but it is on my list. If you ever find yourself up north go check it out. Located inside a B&B this little café offers their famous pancake variations and other diabetes inducing delicacies. Don’t quarrel with the owner though – you made get publicly told off on his twitter account @whitemoosecafe .

268, 272 N Circular Rd, Phibsborough, Dublin, D07 DT61, Ireland

Crabby Joe's

If you ever find yourself hungry in Howth you have two options: Seafood and seafood. If you want to sit down for your meal I would – and I cannot stress this enough – recommend the restaurant with the name Crabby Joe’s. This little joint might not look like it offers great food, but it totally does. Last time I was there with my mum, we each had like 12 oysters and a whole lobster with a huge mountain of fries each and it was CHEAP AS HELL. And also delicious.

14 W Pier, Howth, Dublin, Irland

The Workman's Club

This is not only a great pub, but it also serves delicious Burgers! On the roof! Don’t miss out on this quirkily decorated greasy rooftop-burger-joint.

10 Wellington Quay, Temple Bar, Dublin, D02 VX36, Irland

photo © https://theworkmansclub.com/spaces/the-rooftop-terrace/

Pit Bro's BBQ

I don’t think I’ve EVER had such delicious BBQ. I also don’t think I’ve ever eaten less graceful. 5/5 would ALWAYS want to go.

84-88 George’s Street Upper, Dublin, A96 W8W9, Irland

Mama's Revenge Burrito

Another great burrito place! Try it ;)

12 Leinster St S, Dublin 2, D02 H367, Irland

Little Ass Burrito

This is hands down the best burrito in town. Literally all of the options are great, I do have fond memories of the chorizo burrito though ;)

Location 1 Temple Bar Music Centre, Curved St, Temple Bar

Location 2 10 Rathgar Rd, Rathmines

Tolteca

Classic fast food Mexican restaurant. Chipotle vibes, Pretty good ;)

21 Suffolk St, Dublin 2, D02 WA48, Irland

Pablo Picante

Don’t go for those fast food places if you can go to independently owned restaurants. Check out Pablo Picante: The interior is quirky and colorful but most importantly, the burrito is freaking goooooood. Next to the restaurant Is the Torta Shop. Here you can get Tortas – traditional Mexican-style gourmet sandwiches.

4 Clarendon Market, Dublin Southside, Dublin, D02 AV84, Irland

Murphy's Ice Cream

It’s a hot day and you need to cool down? Or you’re just in the mood for the best ice cream in the entire world? Head down to Wicklow Street and get yourself a flavourful treat. Favourite flavours include: Gin, Sea Salt and Brown Bread Ice Cream (fucking trust me on this one!)

And hey, if you ever find yourself in Dingle, check out the original shop there!

27 Wicklow St, Dublin 2, D02 WN51, Irland

The Rolling Donut

Scene. You're on O’Connell Street. Your stomach starts rumbling and you need to find a quick snack. As you walk up the streets you spot what looks like an old fashioned cinema ticket booth. As you get closer you smell the heavenly scent of fat and sugar. It’s a donut booth. You’re saved.

O’Connell Street Lower, North City, Dublin, D01 P2C7, Irland
