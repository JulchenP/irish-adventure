Fancy a pint?

Feel that itch in your throat? You need a good ale!

↓

Hear that? That's a Guinness calling for you!

Instead of opting for the touristy choice that is Temple Bar, why don't you check out some of these more local gems? Most pubs offer a great selection of beers, ales and also whisk(e)ys. And they aren't as pricey as Temple Bar.

The different kind of beers you should try are:

  • Stout (e.g. Guinness)
  • Irish Red Ale (e.g. Kilkenny Red Ale)
  • Irish Lager (e.g. Harp)
  • Craft Beer (They have a lot of options, just try whatever's on tap)

Another popular drink choice is cider! The sour and fruity drink is a great alternative if you don't feel like drinking a heavy beer. It's not everybody's taste though.

Classic Pubs

J.W. Sweetman

This was my favorite pub back in 2014. They have a great selection of beers and as they're right on the Quay you can enjoy a beautiful water view while sipping on your drink.

1-2 Burgh Quay, Dublin 2, D02 F243, Ireland

Mary’s Bar & Hardware

Never heard Bar & Hardware in a shop name before? Well, now you have. This little bar offers an excellent selection of beer and if you ever run out of batteries, you’ll get those here too! „Interested in hearing a joke about our hardware section? Sorry, it’s under construction!“

8 Wicklow St, Dublin 2, D02 AX90, Irland

The Duke

Ideally situated close to the city centre on historic Duke street, this old watering hole, which takes it’s title from the little street named after the 2nd Duke of Grafton, has been providing liquid refreshment and sustenance to the people of Dublin since 1822.

9 Duke St, Dublin 2, D02 NR76, Irland

The Brew Dock

Next to the central bus station you’ll find this nice and cosy pub. They have a huge range of imported and local bottled beers.

1 Amiens St, Mountjoy, Dublin 1, Irland

The Woolshed Baa & Grill

Have never been for food, but can totally recommend for watching football games with you peers. They have stadium-like seats and the „Dublin German Speaker“ go there quite often.

198-200, Parnell St, Centre, Dublin, D01 K5N8, Irland

For Whisky Lovers

Dingle Whiskey Bar

Hands down my favourite spot to drink whisky in Dublin. It’s such a quaint bar, loads of barrel parts on the wall and the bartenders are a joy to talk to. They also offer tastings which I haven’t had the chance to check out but would totally do it! Selection of whiskies is great. It’s ajoined to the Porterhouse Brew Pub, which is nice enough too.

44 Nassau St, Dublin 2, Irland

Cafés

Bewley's Café

Ever wondered what the perfect cup of tea tastes like? Wonder no more! In the (I have to admit touristy) world-famous Bewley’s Café you can choose between a selection of teas and enjoy a scone with it too! It’s been there for ages, my mum used to go there when she was still in college.

119 Grafton Street, Dublin, D02 E620, Ireland

Café Nero

Are you craving a dose of caffeine? Forget about Starbucks. Café Nero is literally just another international Coffee House Chain, but their coffee tastes better, it’s cheaper and the scones are nice too! Give it a shot.

Litteraly anywhere in town. Skip Starkbucks.

Café en Seine

Want to take a break from Irish pubs? How about fleeing Paris even it’s just for a coffee or an enjoyable jazz night? (Great place to take a date to, too!)

40 Dawson St, Dublin, Irland
